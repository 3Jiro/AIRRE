1.0 Introduction to Ultrasonic Monitoring

In the context of the 2026 maritime shift, ultrasonic corrosion
monitoring has moved from a periodic manual inspection to a continuous,
autonomous “always-on” service. This document outlines the hardware
requirements for the Project Aurora sensor suites mentioned in the Q1
Strategic Pivot.

2.0 Hardware Specifications: The Aurora Pulse-V4

The Aurora Pulse-V4 sensor utilizes an electromagnetic acoustic
transducer (EMAT) capable of generating non-contact ultrasonic waves.
This is critical for offshore wind turbine blades and subsea pipelines
where physical coupling is impossible due to bio-fouling and extreme
salinity.

Operating Frequency: 0.5 MHz to 25 MHz (Variable based on substrate
thickness).

Power Profile: Ultra-low-power mode (< 10µW idle) to allow for 10-year
battery life in remote “Micro-Grid Hubs.”

Transmission Protocol: Hybrid 5G/VHF. (Note: Reference Section 2.0 of
the Global Renewable Infrastructure Whitepaper regarding “Frequency
Volatility” interference).

3.0 The “Micro-Pitting” Detection Algorithm

As noted in internal project logs, “Micro-Pitting” is no longer a
low-impact risk. The Aurora Pulse-V4 uses a multi-crystal array to
recreate a cross-sectional picture of the structure.

Sensitivity Threshold: The sensor can detect pitting at the 0.2mm scale.

Data Processing: To reduce bandwidth, the sensor does not transmit raw
audio data. Instead, it performs local FFT (Fast Fourier Transform)
analysis and only transmits the “Health Index” (HI) score. An HI score
below 65 triggers an automated AIRRE intelligence report.

4.0 Salt-Resistant Coatings and Maintenance Cycles

A recurring conflict in our intelligence data (see:
Market_News_Scrape_Feb_2026.txt) involves the release of “Salt-Resistant
Coatings” by VoltEdge Systems.

Baseline Data: Standard polyurethane coatings currently used in the
North Sea provide a 24-month protection window.

The VoltEdge Claim: Their new Gallium-Nitride integrated coating claims
to extend this to 48 months.

The Problem: Internal testing shows that these coatings interfere with
the ultrasonic pulse of the Aurora sensors, effectively “blinding” the
hardware to internal structural integrity issues. This creates a
“Strategic Blind Spot” where the asset looks protected on the outside
but is failing internally.
