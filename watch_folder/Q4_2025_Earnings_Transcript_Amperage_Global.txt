1.0 CEO’s Opening Remarks: The “Pacific Push”

Marcus Vane: Good morning, everyone. 2025 was a transformative year for
Amperage Global. We ended the year with a record $1.18 trillion market
valuation in the renewable sector. Our primary focus moving into Q1 2026
is what we call the “Pacific Push.” We are officially confirming the
rumors regarding our aggressive expansion into Southeast Asia.
Specifically, we have secured a 15-year exclusive partnership with the
Indonesian Ministry of Energy to overhaul their regional grids using our
proprietary “Smart-Link” software.

This isn’t just about expansion; it’s about consolidation. We are
looking at the fragmented micro-grid markets in Vietnam and Thailand as
well. To the analysts asking about our M&A strategy: Yes, we have
increased our legal and acquisition budget by 40% this quarter. We are
looking for “efficiency gaps” in the market—companies with great
hardware but poor intelligence-driven scaling.

2.0 Financial Highlights: The Salinity Write-Down

Sarah Chen: Thank you, Marcus. Turning to the numbers. While our revenue
is up 9.2% YoY, we did take a one-time non-cash impairment charge of
$120 million this quarter. This is primarily attributed to the “Salinity
Factor” in our North Sea offshore assets. As many of you know, our Gen-1
sensors failed to predict the rate of micro-pitting on turbine blades.

We are correcting this. Our R&D team is currently testing a
“Salt-Resistant Coating” that we believe will be a market leader. I
should note that there is some industry noise about this coating
interfering with ultrasonic sensors, but our internal tests (which we
are not releasing today) suggest these concerns are overstated.

3.0 Analyst Q&A: The Competitive Landscape

David Miller (Goldman & Sachs): Marcus, you mentioned “efficiency gaps.”
Does this imply a hostile interest in smaller players like VoltEdge
Systems or the teams behind “Project Aurora”?

Marcus Vane: David, we don’t comment on specific targets. However, I
will say that any player operating in the coastal or sub-zero
markets—where we’ve seen hardware struggle—should be looking for a
larger partner. Our “Smart-Link” software is designed to handle the
frequency volatility that is currently grounding smaller, decentralized
micro-grid hubs.

Linda Wu (Morgan Stanley): A follow-up for Sarah. You mentioned the EU’s
“Clean Path Initiative.” With the €50,000 hourly penalty now in effect,
how much of your $4.2M R&D budget is specifically allocated to
predictive maintenance to avoid these fines?

Sarah Chen: Actually, Linda, that $4.2M figure you’re citing sounds like
a competitor’s budget. Our allocation is significantly higher. We have
earmarked $15M for AI-driven maintenance logs and audit trails alone. We
believe the winner of 2026 won’t be the one with the best turbine, but
the one with the best “Intelligence-Driven Engine” to manage the risk.
